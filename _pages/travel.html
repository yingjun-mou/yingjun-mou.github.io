Travel is one of my passions - and I take it seriously. For each travel opportunity, I will do research of the destination before departure in order to maximize what I learn from the trip. During the visit, I am inquisitive especially about the local languages/dialects, architectures and arts, cuisine, and the geography.

Back to the time when I was an architecture student (yes, I had an architectural design background before studying computer science), I was lucky to get the opportunity to study abroad in Italy in 2015, to have immersive education about western architectural theory and history, specialized in Renaissance, Baroque, and Italian urbanism. I also went to Brazil and Tunis to research the urban development issues. Back in 2017, I interned for one of the Japanese star architects Junya Ishigami in Tokyo, Japan. All these experiences have shaped my mind to be humble and willing to appreciate the cultural differences among the diverse groups of mankind.

Starting from Thailand in 2008 to Brazil in 2019, so far I have collected visa stamps from 22 different countries/regions across the globe with all continents except Antarctica visited. My next destination is a somewhere in South America. If you have any travel experiences to share, hit me up!
